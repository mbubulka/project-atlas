# Community Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Project Atlas is committed to fostering an inclusive, respectful, and welcoming community. We are dedicated to creating a safe space for all contributors, users, and stakeholders, regardless of background, identity, or experience level.

This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for respectful interaction and the consequences for unacceptable behavior.

---

## Our Values

### 1. **Respect**
- Treat all community members with dignity and respect
- Value diverse perspectives, experiences, and expertise
- Recognize the contributions of all participants
- Listen actively and engage constructively in disagreement

### 2. **Inclusivity**
- Welcome contributions from people of all backgrounds
- Create accessible spaces for participation (clear documentation, inclusive language)
- Support newer members and contributors
- Actively work to reduce barriers to participation

### 3. **Academic Integrity**
- Give proper credit and attribution for ideas and contributions
- Cite sources and acknowledge the work of others
- Be transparent about limitations and assumptions
- Engage in good-faith scientific discussion

### 4. **Safety & Professionalism**
- Maintain a harassment-free environment for all
- Use inclusive and professional language
- Respect privacy and personal boundaries
- Focus on the work, not personal attacks

---

## Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

✅ **Be respectful** — Interact with courtesy and consideration, even in disagreement
✅ **Be constructive** — Provide actionable feedback focused on ideas, not individuals
✅ **Be collaborative** — Work together toward shared goals of advancing the project
✅ **Be transparent** — Acknowledge limitations, uncertainties, and conflicts of interest
✅ **Be inclusive** — Use language and practices that welcome people of all backgrounds
✅ **Be honest** — Represent ideas accurately and disclose contributors/sources
✅ **Be patient** — Understand that others may have different communication styles or learning curves
✅ **Assume good intent** — Interpret ambiguous messages charitably before escalating concerns

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are **not tolerated** in this community:

❌ **Harassment & Discrimination** — Any form of harassment, discrimination, or abuse based on:
  - Identity (race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, etc.)
  - Experience level or background
  - Appearance or age
  - Nationality or immigration status

❌ **Hateful or Violent Language** — Slurs, threats, or dehumanizing language toward any group

❌ **Sexual Harassment** — Unwanted sexual advances, comments, or imagery

❌ **Bad-Faith Attacks** — Personal attacks, deliberate misrepresentation, or ad hominem arguments

❌ **Doxxing & Privacy Violations** — Sharing private information without consent, or attempting to identify someone without permission

❌ **Trolling & Disruption** — Deliberately disruptive behavior intended to derail conversations or waste contributors' time

❌ **Plagiarism & Misattribution** — Taking credit for others' work or failing to acknowledge sources

❌ **Abuse of Power** — Maintainers or moderators using their position to harm or intimidate community members

❌ **Spam** — Excessive self-promotion or off-topic messages that clutter discussions

## Reporting & Enforcement

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Contact the maintainer directly** — Email: [Project Atlas Maintainer Email]
   - Include details of the incident (what happened, when, who was involved)
   - Provide context or supporting evidence if available
   - Indicate the impact and your desired resolution

2. **Private channels** — For sensitive reports, communication will be kept confidential

3. **No retaliation** — Report concerns without fear of retaliation or negative consequences

### Response & Investigation

The maintainer will:
- Acknowledge receipt of your report within 2 business days
- Investigate the incident promptly and fairly
- Maintain confidentiality to the extent possible
- Communicate the outcome to the reporter
- Document incidents to identify patterns

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:
- **Warning** — First-time minor violations; clear expectation for future behavior
- **Temporary suspension** — Removal from project discussions/contributions (e.g., 30 days)
- **Permanent ban** — Repeated violations or severe misconduct; permanent removal from community

The severity of consequences depends on:
- Nature and impact of the violation
- Contributor's history
- Willingness to correct behavior
- Safety considerations

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- **All project spaces** — GitHub discussions, issues, pull requests, wiki, project board
- **External forums** — Conferences, meetups, or online forums where Project Atlas is discussed
- **Contributor interactions** — Communications between contributors both inside and outside project spaces
- **Related social media** — Comments on posts explicitly about Project Atlas

This Code of Conduct does **not** apply to:
- Private personal communications unrelated to the project
- Off-topic spaces or conversations

## Special Considerations for Academic Research

### Research Ethics
- Acknowledge funding sources and potential conflicts of interest
- Be transparent about research limitations and assumptions
- Give proper credit to collaborators, data sources, and prior work
- Follow institutional and regulatory requirements (IRB, data governance)

### Feedback & Criticism
- Scientific disagreement and constructive criticism are welcome
- Critique ideas and methods, not individuals
- Engage with evidence and reasoning, not rhetoric

### Inclusion in Military Community
- Recognize the diversity of military experiences and backgrounds
- Avoid stereotypes or assumptions about service members
- Be sensitive to trauma, loss, and difficult transitions
- Respect privacy and confidentiality of user data
